The National formed in 1999 with the current members Matt Berninger, brothers Aaaron and Bryce Dessner and brothers Bryan and Scott Davendorf. The band has a discography of seven albums, each building off the sound of the last to create intricate and emotional music. The National builds off of tones of sorrow and pain while exploring the troubles of societal life. Berninger's bass-baritone vocals range from morose to bright, often in the same song, creating a dynamic that works in harmony with the other band member's guitar, bass and drum heavy rock music. The national are seen by many as one of the "premier" indie-alternative bands in the music industry, and their unique and innovative sound and onstage energy sets them apart as one of the most-successful bands of the 2000s.
